DoctorsNote

DoctorsNote is an iOS app I built with my team in my Software Engineering Class (CS 307, Purdue University) in Spring 2020. This project used Swift, AWS, and MariaDB.


❮ ❯



The goal of DoctorsNote is to create a community of support through this platform and promote patient mental health and wellness.

Problem Motivation

Doctors need a method to communicate with patients outside the office. Patients have questions that can be answered through a messaging service rather than through an in-person appointment. However, HIPAA adds restrictions preventing use of normal messaging platforms. Other HIPAA-compliant services are expensive and lack support groups. Therefore, my team wanted to provide a secure, HIPAA-compliant app for doctors and patients to communicate in a timely manner without fearing for a patient's privacy.

App Features

  • Secure messaging between patients and doctors on the iOS app
  • Hospital administrator capabilities through the connected web application
  • Appointment scheduling
  • Medication reminder setup
  • Access to virtual support groups

Looking Forward

Future goals of DoctorsNote include adopting support group moderation, adding more hospital administrator features, and releasing the project to the public domain, which can give lower budget health systems a low cost or free alternative to commercial solutions.

This project began in January 2020, before the extensive spread of the COVID-19 pandemic. However, the current situation of the world has brought another potential way for DoctorsNote to be utilized, as it provides a secure, contactless method for patients and their health specialists to communicate.
